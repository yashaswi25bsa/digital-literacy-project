SOCIAL MEDIA CHECKLIST FOR COLLEGE STUDENTS: DO'S AND DON'TS

THE DO'S:
[ ] 1. Do audit your digital footprint. Assume that future employers, internship coordinators, and professors will search your name.
[ ] 2. Do review your privacy settings frequently. Ensure you know exactly who can see your photos, posts, and personal information.
[ ] 3. Do use platforms to build a professional network. Follow industry leaders, join relevant college groups, and connect with alumni.
[ ] 4. Do pause before you post. Apply the "Billboard Rule": If you wouldn't want it on a massive billboard in the middle of campus, do not post it.
[ ] 5. Do set screen time limits. Protect your mental health and academic schedule by managing the time you spend scrolling.

THE DON'TS:
[ ] 1. Don't post evidence of unprofessional or rule-breaking behavior, such as academic dishonesty or underage drinking. 
[ ] 2. Don't complain about professors, classmates, or university administration publicly. If you need to vent, do it offline.
[ ] 3. Don't overshare sensitive personal details. Avoid posting your daily class schedule, exact dorm location, or real-time whereabouts.
[ ] 4. Don't engage in heated online arguments, trolling, or harassment. Always keep your public interactions respectful.
[ ] 5. Don't share news or articles without verifying the source first to avoid contributing to the spread of misinformation.
