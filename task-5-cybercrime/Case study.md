## Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud in Online Marketplaces





UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a prevalent cybercrime where scammers deceive individuals into authorizing direct bank transfers under false pretenses. This often occurs on peer-to-peer marketplaces where people frequently buy or sell second-hand goods, such as textbooks or electronics.



In a typical scenario, the crime unfolds in four distinct steps:



Initial Contact: A college student lists a used laptop for sale online. A scammer immediately contacts them, expressing urgent interest and agreeing to the asking price without negotiation.



The Hook: The scammer claims they are out of town and insists on paying immediately via a UPI app (like Google Pay, Paytm, or PhonePe) to "reserve" the item.



The Trap: Instead of sending money, the scammer sends a "Collect Request" or a customized QR code to the student's phone.



The Execution: The scammer manipulates the student over a phone call, falsely instructing them that they must enter their confidential UPI PIN to receive the payment.



College students looking for quick cash and elderly users are frequently targeted because they may be momentarily distracted or unfamiliar with the exact mechanics of digital payment requests. The consequences are immediate and severe: the victim suffers instant financial loss—which is notoriously difficult to reverse once authorized—alongside significant emotional distress and a lasting loss of trust in digital platforms.
